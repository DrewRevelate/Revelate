# Thought-Leadership Content Strategy
## Revelate Operations - Series B RevOps Consulting

**Strategy Overview:**
Position Drew Lambert as the go-to authority for Series B SaaS companies navigating Salesforce modernization and revenue operations challenges. Focus on operator-to-operator content that demonstrates deep technical expertise while remaining accessible to non-technical executives.

**Target Audience:**
- Primary: Series B SaaS Founders, COOs, VP of Revenue Operations ($10-50M ARR)
- Secondary: RevOps Managers, Sales Operations Leaders, CFOs preparing for Series C

**Content Pillars:**
1. **Salesforce Modernization** (40% of content)
2. **Revenue Operations Architecture** (30% of content)
3. **Series B Growth Challenges** (20% of content)
4. **Operator Insights & Behind-the-Scenes** (10% of content)

## Content Templates

### Blog Post Structure

**Introduction (150 words)**
- Hook: Startling statistic or relatable pain point
- Context: Why this matters for Series B SaaS
- Promise: What readers will learn

**Body (1,200-2,000 words)**
- 3-5 main sections with H2 headings
- Use bullet points and numbered lists
- Include 1-2 real examples/case studies
- Add data/statistics where possible

**Conclusion (100 words)**
- Summary of key takeaways
- Clear CTA (book call, download resource, take quiz)

**SEO Checklist:**
- [ ] Target keyword in title
- [ ] Target keyword in first paragraph
- [ ] 2-3 related keywords throughout
- [ ] Internal links to 2+ pages
- [ ] External links to 1-2 authoritative sources
- [ ] Meta description (150-160 characters)
- [ ] Alt text on all images

### LinkedIn Post Formula

**Hook (First 2 Lines)**
- Surprising statement or question
- Must grab attention in feed

**Body (3-5 Short Paragraphs)**
- Expand on the hook
- Provide 3 specific insights or examples
- Use line breaks for readability

**CTA**
- Ask a question
- Encourage comments
- Link to blog post (in comments)

**Example:**
```
Your board wants forecast accuracy.

Your Salesforce gives them guesses.

Here's the real problem:

→ Manual data entry creates lag
→ Integration gaps lose context
→ Historical data is a mess
→ Territory changes break reporting

I've seen this at 50+ Series B companies.

The fix isn't a Salesforce rebuild.
It's a 6-week architecture intervention.

What's the #1 forecast accuracy challenge your team faces?
```
