## 1. Competitive Landscape

### Market Structure

**Large Firms (Deloitte, Accenture, IBM)**
- **Strengths:** Brand recognition, comprehensive service offerings, global reach
- **Weaknesses:** High costs ($200K+ projects), lengthy timelines (6-12 months), junior staff on implementations
- **Not Direct Competitors:** Target enterprise clients, different price point entirely

**Mid-Size Boutique Firms (50-500 employees)**
- **Examples:** Soliant Consulting, CloudMasonry, Peeklogic, Candybox CRM, Coastal Cloud
- **Strengths:** Industry specialization, SaaS focus, faster than big firms (6-8 weeks vs 10-12 weeks)
- **Pricing:** Competitive but still project-based, typically $50K-$150K engagements
- **Direct Competitors:** Yes, especially those focusing on Series B SaaS

**Small Agencies (5-50 employees)**
- **Examples:** CrestOps, Routine Automation, Seven Results, Think Beyond
- **Strengths:** Personalized service, agility, direct access to senior consultants
- **Weaknesses:** Limited bandwidth, may not have full technical depth
- **Direct Competitors:** Moderate - compete on personalization but different service model

**Solo/Fractional Consultants**
- **Pricing:** $125-$175/hr for senior consultants in US
- **Service Model:** Retainer or project-based
- **Weaknesses (per research):** "Cannot cover all dimensions of RevOps" - lack breadth
- **Direct Competitors:** Yes - this is Drew's category

### SaaS-Specific Consultants

**Candybox CRM**
- Positioning: "Holistic RevOps approach" for SaaS companies
- Focus: Scalable solutions, hands-on service, adaptable systems
- Notable: Addresses Salesforce technical debt specifically for growth-stage SaaS

**Peeklogic**
- Positioning: Integration specialists (Jira, NetSuite, Azure DevOps)
- Target: SaaS companies with complex tech stacks

**CloudMasonry**
- Positioning: Comprehensive Salesforce + CPQ specialists
- Strength: Communication around project risks/rewards
