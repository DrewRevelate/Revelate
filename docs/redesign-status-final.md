---

#### 2. Prepare Testing Materials (30 min)
- [ ] Copy testing script from design-thinking doc
- [ ] Create Feedback Capture Grid spreadsheet/doc
- [ ] Set up screen recording (if remote): Zoom, Loom, or browser extension

---

#### 3. Run Tests (3-4 hours total)
**Per session (30 minutes each):**
- 5 min: Intro + context
- 15 min: 4 testing tasks (observe behavior, ask them to think aloud)
- 10 min: Follow-up questions

**Tasks to run:**
1. "Can you figure out what I do in 10 seconds?" (Show homepage, count to 10, ask)
2. "You need Salesforce help. Would you book a call?" (Let them explore, watch behavior)
3. "What would you want to know before booking?" (Note gaps)
4. "What's your first impression?" (Show 3 seconds, hide, ask reaction)

**Questions to ask:**
- "When you see 'I take 2-3 clients at a time,' what does that signal?"
- "Did you click any 'Learn more' sections? Why or why not?"
- "How does this compare to other consultants you've considered?"
- "Scale 1-10, how confident would you feel booking a call?"

---

#### 4. Analyze Patterns (1 hour)
- What did 3+ people struggle with?
- What surprised them (good or bad)?
- What questions kept coming up?
- What convinced them vs what didn't?

**Look for:**
- Confusion points
- Missing information
- Trust signals that worked/didn't work
- Visual elements that helped/hurt

---

#### 5. Fix Top 3 Friction Points (2-4 hours)
Based on patterns, make targeted fixes.

**Don't redesign** - just remove friction.

---

#### 6. Retest + Ship (1 hour)
- Show 2 users the fixes
- Confirm improvement
- Deploy to production
- Announce update

## Success Metrics (From Design Thinking)

After testing, you should be able to answer:

### Validation Metrics
- [ ] Can 5/7 users explain what you do in 10 seconds?
- [ ] Does trust rating average 8+ out of 10?
- [ ] Does "Principal-Led" signal expertise (not "too small")?
- [ ] Does restrained aesthetic feel professional?
- [ ] Do visitors use progressive disclosure features?

### Riskiest Assumptions to Test
1. **"Principal-Led" signals expertise** - Does "2-3 clients at a time" build trust or concern?
2. **Progressive disclosure is desired** - Do they click "Learn more" or want everything visible?
3. **Restrained aesthetic feels trustworthy** - Professional or empty/unfinished?
4. **7-second comprehension works** - Can they grasp each section's point that fast?
5. **Concrete outcomes > industry stats** - What convinced them you could help?
